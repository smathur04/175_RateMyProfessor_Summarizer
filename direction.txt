We stick to pulling the top k (k will likely be 5) positive and negative words
from our logistic regression tf-idf model the same way we already have been.

Then, since this is more of a last minute development we pick about 10-20 
professors with a good amount of reviews, let's say 8+ for this example. 
For each review we multiply the absolute value of the sentiment score by our 
own calculated “worthwhile” score which gives a number based on the length 
of the review, a number prioritizing good grades that left bad reviews or
bad grades that left good reviews, and maybe another metric or two. We 
then pick the top 3 reviews that have the highest resulting product score 
so these are the 3 most worthwhile and strongly sentiment carrying. 

These three reviews are simply appended to each other as 1 large review and 
the llm is prompted to summarize these three highest impact reviews into 
one summary and we provide our top k again and tell it that if it sees 
those words or very close synonyms to ensure the summary carries forward those terms.

Then we re-run our model on both the collection of the three reviews provided 
to the llm and get its sentiment score and also run it on our summary to get 
its sentiment score and analyze how close together these are. This shows if 
the model is hallucinating and/or if it is successfully carrying forward what
matters in its summary. We could also do a plain average sentiment score 
for all the reviews for that professor and show that next to this too to 
examine how our process focuses things on more important key issues and 
perhaps cuts out outliers/noise.
